What this review covers

This audio session breaks down Lesson 15 into a practical story of identifying and managing organizational risk — from initial discovery through analysis, prioritization, and response.

  • Risk identification: recognizing vulnerabilities, threats, operational weaknesses, and system dependencies across mission-essential functions.
  • Risk analysis: explaining the difference between qualitative scoring (high/medium/low) and quantitative metrics like SLE, ARO, and ALE.
  • Business Impact Analysis (BIA): determining critical processes, Maximum Tolerable Downtime (MTD), Recovery Time Objective (RTO), and Recovery Point Objective (RPO).
  • Risk responses: walking through avoid, mitigate, transfer, and accept — when each is appropriate and how compensating controls fit in.
  • Residual vs inherent risk: understanding how controls change exposure and how leadership sets risk appetite and tolerance.
  • Risk register & KRIs: capturing risks, owners, severity, heat-map classification, and using Key Risk Indicators to detect increases in exposure.
  • Assessment types: differentiating between ad hoc, one-time, recurring, and continuous assessments.
  • Enterprise Risk Management (ERM): mapping organizational risk posture to strategic objectives and compliance requirements.
